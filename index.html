Understand Dementia

Awareness Helps Families Take Action Earlier

Dementia describes changes in memory, thinking, communication, and reasoning that interfere with daily life. It is caused by disease in the brain and is not a normal part of aging.

🧠

Common Signs

  • Memory loss affecting daily life
  • Difficulty completing familiar tasks
  • Confusion about time or place
  • New problems with words
  • Mood or personality changes
⚕️

Other Causes of Memory Loss

  • Depression
  • Medication side effects
  • Infections
  • Hearing or vision problems
  • Sleep problems or poor nutrition
❤️

Why Early Diagnosis Matters

  • Get an accurate diagnosis
  • Access support sooner
  • Understand the disease
  • Plan for care and lifestyle changes
  • Improve quality of life

Acknowledgements

Educational Resources & Special Thanks

We gratefully acknowledge the Aging & Disability Resource Center (ADRC) of Dane County and Nyob Zoo TV for developing the Hmong dementia educational materials and producing the award-winning film “My Mother (Kuv Nam): A Hmong Story on Dementia.”

Their leadership in creating culturally and linguistically appropriate dementia education has helped communities across Wisconsin increase awareness, reduce stigma, and encourage early conversations about dementia.

This initiative is presented through the collaboration of the Milwaukee County Department of Health & Human Services – Aging & Disabilities Services, the Aging & Disability Resource Center (ADRC) of Milwaukee County, Milwaukee Community Crossroads (MCC), the Alzheimer's Association Wisconsin Chapter, and the Wisconsin Chinese Chamber of Commerce (WCCC) to expand dementia awareness and support Hmong families throughout Milwaukee County and beyond.
